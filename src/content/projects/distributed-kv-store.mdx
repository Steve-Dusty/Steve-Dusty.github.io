A from-scratch implementation of the Raft consensus protocol powering a distributed key-value store. Supports linearizable reads, log compaction, and dynamic membership changes.

## Highlights

- Full Raft implementation including leader election, log replication, and snapshotting
- gRPC transport layer with configurable timeouts
- Benchmarked at ~50k writes/sec on a 3-node cluster

## Implementation Notes

Building a consensus protocol from the paper is one of those projects that seems straightforward until you hit the edge cases. Paxos is notoriously difficult to understand — Raft was designed to be understandable, and the paper is genuinely readable — but "understandable" doesn't mean "easy to implement correctly."

The hardest bug I hit was a race condition in leader election when network partitions healed. The key insight: you need to reset election timers *before* processing the AppendEntries RPC, not after.

**Takeaways:**

1. Property-based testing (with `proptest`) caught bugs that unit tests missed
2. Deterministic simulation testing is worth the upfront investment
3. The TLA+ spec is invaluable for cross-checking your implementation
